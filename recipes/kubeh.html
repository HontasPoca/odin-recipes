Kub'eh - Soup with pastry balls filled with meat

Description

One of the best dishes and a favorite by me and many many Israelis. The dish's variety of greens, middle eastern spices and meat keep the dish in a moderate level of health prioritization, while also giving you a taste that sends you to the essence of what many Israelis conceptualize of being Israeli.

Ingredients

  • Kub'eh package 'Jasmin' tagged
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 6 sliced garlic tooth
  • 5 coarsely sliced whole Zuccinis
  • Coarsely chopped Celery - whole pack
  • 'Mangold' Spinach package
  • 1 Coriander package
  • 1 Parsley package
  • 2 litter preheated water
  • Salt&Pepper
  • Hwyag
  • Half a glass Lemon juice
  • Quarter a glass Canola oil

Steps

  1. We heat up oil and the chopped onions in a big pot, to a golden tone.
  2. We add the rest of the prepared vegetables and spices to the pot, excluding the lemon juice and the spices.
  3. Fry the materials in the pot for a 5 minute fry
  4. Add the preheated 2 liters of water.
  5. We lower the flame power and letting it cook for 30 minute cook.
  6. Now, we add the lemon juice to the stew, sampling and balancing for our own taste.
  7. Next, is the time for putting the Kub'eh balls into the hot soup, til cooked.
  8. Wait 20 minutes for a quick mingling
  9. Getting ready for a 4 to 5 meatballs dish, and a soup. In the meantime-let the stew cook for another 10 minutes.
  10. Throw yourself some meatballs on a soup, and enjoy one of the best dishes of the middle-east food culture.
